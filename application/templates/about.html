Project Description

Diet high in fat and sugar is particularly dangerous for women. In addition to number of metabolic abnormalities, diet-induced obesity correlate with development of polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) in women(Hoeger, et al, 2012). While cysts development in ovary is hallmark of this disorder, the mechanism for its development is still unknown. Even more alarming, identification of cysts in ovarian tissue is currently conducted by visual examination of histological slides by experienced pathologist. Previously, our lab havedeveloped a rat model of diet-induced PCOS by feeding adolescent female rats a high-fat diet supplemented by a 32% liquid sucrose solution (HFHS diet). After 15 weeks on the diet, 75% of animals became acyclic and exhibited classic hallmarks of PCOS such as a large number of cysts in the ovary (Fig 1), increased androgens, insulin insensitivity, and acyclicity (Roberts, et al, 2017).

Over the last few years we collected images of rat ovaries and manually quantify presence of cysts in HFHS group and healthy follicles in control group. Our findings indicate that HFHS diet modify development of ovarian follicles in rat ovary. Animal on a diet hassignificantly less indicators of ovulation (corpus luteum, CL) and significantly more cysts(Volk, et al, 2017).However, the main bottleneck of this project is processing of ovarian histology which is currently is done by manually inspecting every follicular image.The goal of this project is to extracting images of individual follicular structures (healthy follicles, unhealthy follicles and cysts) using machine learning algorithm. We will be collecting images of individual follicular structures and use as a training set in a machine learning model to automatically classify those follicular structures.
